ELC Calibration

In the ELC Calibration dialog you can:

  • Calibrate the electronic level of the HiPer VR/HiPer HR receiver in the Level area.
  • Calibrate the magnetic compass of the HiPer VR/HiPer HR receiver in 3D in the Compass area.
  • Calibrate the magnetic compass of the HiPer VR/HiPer HR receiver in the horizontal plane in the Compass area.

Calibration is performed independently and sequentially. After all three calibrations are finished, the receiver HIMU is ready to use.

There are the general recommendations for carrying out the calibration of the HIMU:

  • The calibration is performed in the field, after that the receiver tracked the satellites and determines a position.
  • The connection with a controller is established via Bluetooth or Wi-Fi.
  • Before calibration it is necessary to turn on and/or attach all equipment which will be used for the survey. For RTK survey, receive the corrections data using the desired modem and obtain the Fixed solution.
    • if you use UHF or FH modem, connect the radio antenna;
    • if you will use UHF or FH modem, turn on the radio modem;
    • if you will use both batteries for the survey, attach the removable battery,
    • do not use an external power source.

If the calibration data are no longer valid, the icon appears.

Calibrate Level

To take an accurate level and compass reading, you need to perform the electronic level calibration procedure.

1. Set a tripod with a universal tribrach and tribrach adapter. Level the tribrach. Set the receiver on a tribrach adapter with 100 mm aluminum prism spacer. Eliminate any vibrations during the calibration. The receiver will track the satellites and determining a position.

2. Click the Calibrate button in the Level field.
3. Wait for 30 seconds until the HIMU (Level & Compass) dialog is closed.
4. When the calibration succeeds, the Last Calibration Time (UTC) field will show a new date and time in the Level area. If the calibration fails, the field will show the date and time of the last successful calibration and the icon.

 

Calibrate Compass in 3D

To take an accurate compass reading, you need to perform the compass calibration procedure for each location before starting the survey. Make sure to calibrate the level before calibrating the compass:

1. Hold the receiver flat with the GNSS antenna radome pointed up, and the LED display panel pointed to you.
2. Click the Calibrate button in the Compass field.
3. Rotate the receiver 360 degrees in the vertical plane along the X axis (Step 1).

 

4. Rotate the receiver about 45 degrees to the left in the vertical plane along the Y axis and rotate the receiver 360 degrees in the vertical plane along the X axis (Step 2).
5. Repeat operation described in the previous stage (number 4) until the receiver completes a full circle (i.e., 360°) around the X axis (Step 3, Step 4).
6. When the calibration succeeds, the Last Calibration Time (UTC) field shows a new date and time in the Compass area. If the calibration fails, the field shows the date and time of the last successful calibration and the icon.

Calibrate Compass in the horizontal plane

To take accurate compass reading, you need to perform the compass calibration procedure in the horizontal plane for each location before starting the survey. First, perform the calibration of the level and calibration of the compass in 3D, then calibrate compass in 2D:

1. Set a tripod with a universal tribrach and tribrach adapter. Level the tribrach. Set the receiver on a tribrach adapter with 100 mm aluminum prism spacer. Maximum accuracy of calibration is possible when the receiver is orthogonal to the horizontal plane. Eliminate any vibrations during the calibration.
2. Make sure that the HiPer VR/HiPer HR receiver displays a no-none solution type.
3. Click the Align button in the Compass area.
4. Turn the receiver clockwise in the horizontal plane. The indicator shows time left until the calibration process is finished.
5. When the calibration succeeds, the Last Alignment Time (UTC) field shows a new date and time in the Compass area. If the calibration fails, the field shows the date and time of the last successful calibration and the icon.

When to Calibrate the ELC Sensor

You will need to calibrate the ELC sensor:

• If the receiver has been off for a long time.
• If the receiver is used for the first time.
• If the survey location changes frequently.
• If the receiver took a shock such as being dropped.
• If the temperature changes on 10 degrees or more.
• If the receiver was transported by a car or airplane.
• If the receiver was near a strong magnetic object or material, such as a permanent magnet, electromagnet, electric transformer, AC power supply, etc.
• After loading new firmware.
• After clearing the NVRAM.
